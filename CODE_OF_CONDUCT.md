# Roneira AI HIFI Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior Examples

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

#### Professional Communication
- Using welcoming and inclusive language in all interactions
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism and feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and project success
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Providing helpful and constructive feedback in code reviews

#### Technical Collaboration
- Sharing knowledge and expertise openly with team members
- Documenting code and decisions for future contributors
- Following established coding standards and best practices
- Being patient when helping others learn new technologies
- Acknowledging and crediting others' contributions appropriately

#### Community Engagement
- Participating constructively in discussions and decision-making
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them get started
- Mentoring junior developers and new contributors
- Celebrating team achievements and individual successes
- Contributing to a positive and productive work environment

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

#### Harassment and Discrimination
- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment, including persistent unwelcome contact
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission
- Discriminatory jokes, comments, or behavior targeting any protected characteristic

#### Professional Misconduct
- Deliberately introducing bugs, security vulnerabilities, or malicious code
- Plagiarizing code or claiming others' work as your own
- Sharing confidential project information or intellectual property without authorization
- Intentionally disrupting project workflows or development processes
- Refusing to follow established project guidelines and standards

#### Disruptive Behavior
- Engaging in sustained disruption of discussions, meetings, or collaborative work
- Making threats of violence or engaging in violent behavior
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Community Guidelines

### Technical Discussions

**Constructive Feedback**
- Focus on the code, not the person writing it
- Provide specific, actionable suggestions for improvement
- Explain the reasoning behind your feedback
- Acknowledge good practices and clever solutions
- Ask questions when you don't understand something

**Code Reviews**
- Review code thoroughly but with kindness and respect
- Suggest improvements without being condescending
- Learn from others' approaches and be open to different solutions
- Focus on maintainability, security, and performance considerations
- Use collaborative language ("we could" instead of "you should")

### Communication Standards

**Written Communication**
- Use clear, professional language in all project communications
- Avoid sarcasm and jokes that might be misunderstood in text
- Be explicit about your intent to avoid miscommunication
- Use threading and proper channels in communication platforms
- Respect others' time by being concise and relevant

**Meetings and Discussions**
- Come prepared and stay focused on agenda items
- Listen actively and allow others to speak without interruption
- Ask clarifying questions when needed
- Respect scheduled time limits and start/end times
- Follow up on action items and commitments made

### Intellectual Property and Attribution

**Open Source Compliance**
- Respect all open source licenses and attribution requirements
- Do not include proprietary or copyrighted code without proper authorization
- Clearly document any third-party dependencies and their licenses
- Follow project guidelines for external library integration

**Contribution Attribution**
- Give credit where credit is due in commit messages and documentation
- Acknowledge collaborators and reviewers in significant contributions
- Be transparent about code sources and inspirations
- Maintain accurate commit history and authorship information

## Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders and project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or its community in public spaces. Examples include:

**Project Spaces**
- GitHub repositories and all associated features (issues, discussions, wikis)
- Project documentation and websites
- Communication channels (Discord, Slack, mailing lists)
- Development environments and shared resources

**Public Representation**
- Using an official project email address
- Posting via an official social media account

- Speaking at conferences or meetups about the project
- Writing blog posts or articles about the project

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Examples**:
- Inappropriate language in commit messages or comments
- Minor violations of communication guidelines
- First-time infractions of project standards

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media.

**Examples**:
- Persistent inappropriate behavior after correction
- Minor harassment or discriminatory comments
- Violation of technical standards with potential impact on project integrity

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.

**Examples**:
- Serious harassment or threats
- Deliberate disruption of project activities
- Repeated violations after warnings
- Security violations or malicious code contributions

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Examples**:
- Severe harassment, threats, or doxxing
- Repeated serious violations despite previous consequences
- Sharing confidential or proprietary information
- Introducing malicious code with intent to harm

## Appeal Process

Any individual subject to enforcement action has the right to appeal the decision:

**Appeal Process**
- Submit appeal via email within 30 days of the enforcement action
- Include detailed explanation of why you believe the action was inappropriate
- Provide any additional context or evidence relevant to your case
- Appeals are reviewed by maintainers not involved in the original decision
- Decision is communicated within 1-2 weeks with reasoning
